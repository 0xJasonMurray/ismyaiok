Acme University — AI Acceptable Use Policy for Regulated Data

This policy governs the use of approved artificial intelligence (AI) services when processing, storing, or transmitting data that is subject to federal, state, or institutional regulation. By using an approved AI service with regulated data, you agree to all terms below.

1. Scope

This policy applies to all faculty, staff, students, and affiliates of Acme University who use institutionally approved AI services in connection with regulated data, including but not limited to data covered by HIPAA, FERPA, GDPR, and applicable state privacy laws.

2. Approved Services Only

You may only use AI services that have been reviewed and approved by the Office of Information Security and for which an appropriate data agreement (e.g., Business Associate Agreement, Data Processing Agreement) is in place. Use of personal or free-tier accounts of approved services is not covered by institutional agreements and is prohibited for regulated data.

HIPAA

3. HIPAA-Covered Data Obligations

When using an AI service approved under a HIPAA Business Associate Agreement (BAA), you must:

  • Use only the institutional enterprise account — never a personal or free-tier account.
  • Limit input to the minimum necessary protected health information (PHI) required for the task.
  • Not use AI-generated output as a substitute for clinical judgment without appropriate human review.
  • Report any suspected breach or unauthorized disclosure of PHI to the Privacy Officer immediately.
  • Ensure all use is consistent with the patient's authorization or an applicable HIPAA exception.
FERPA

4. FERPA-Covered Data Obligations

When using an AI service approved for FERPA-covered student education records, you must:

  • Use only your institutional (@acme.edu) account — never a personal account.
  • Limit input to only the student data necessary to accomplish a legitimate educational purpose.
  • Not share AI-generated outputs that contain student records with unauthorized parties.
  • Not use student data to train, fine-tune, or otherwise improve any AI model.
  • Comply with all applicable FERPA rights, including student rights to inspect and correct records.

5. General Obligations for All Regulated Data

  • You are responsible for all data you submit to an AI service under your institutional credentials.
  • Do not input more data than necessary to accomplish the task (data minimization).
  • Review all AI-generated output before acting on it — AI systems can produce inaccurate results.
  • Do not use AI services to make final, unreviewed decisions that affect individuals' rights or opportunities.
  • Immediately report any data incidents to security@acme.edu.

6. Consequences of Non-Compliance

Violations of this policy may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment or enrollment, civil or criminal liability, and mandatory reporting to regulatory agencies. Acme University reserves the right to revoke access to approved AI services for individuals who misuse them.

7. Questions

Contact the Office of Information Security at security@acme.edu or visit the IT Security portal for guidance on approved AI services and data handling requirements.

Effective date: May 12, 2026 — Acme University Office of Information Security
